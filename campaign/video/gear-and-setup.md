## Camera

### Option 1: iPhone (Recommended for Budget)
**Cost:** $0 (use current phone)

**Pros:**
- Already owned
- Excellent 4K video quality (iPhone 12+)
- Simple, no learning curve
- Built-in stabilization

**Cons:**
- Limited manual control (exposure, focus)
- No interchangeable lenses (limited macro capability)
- Battery drains quickly during long shoots

**Setup:**
- **Always shoot horizontal (landscape orientation)**
- Use Cinema Mode or Filmic Pro app for manual exposure control ($15 one-time)
- Clean lens before every shot
- Enable 4K 24fps or 30fps (Settings → Camera → Record Video)
- Disable HDR video (can cause color grading issues)
- Lock exposure and focus by tapping and holding on subject

---

## Audio

**CRITICAL:** Audio quality matters MORE than video quality. Viewers will tolerate grainy video but will abandon poor audio.

### Essential: Wired Lavalier Mic
**Cost:** $50-100
**Models:**
- RODE smartLav+ ($80) — plugs into iPhone, excellent quality
- PowerDeWise Lavalier Mic ($20-30) — budget option, USB-C or 3.5mm
- Shure MVL ($50) — compact, iOS/Android compatible

**Setup:**
- Clip to shirt collar, 6-8" below chin
- Hide cable under shirt
- Test levels before recording (should peak at -12dB to -6dB)
- Record in quiet room (bedroom, closet with blankets to dampen sound)

**What to avoid:**
- Built-in camera mic (picks up room echo, handling noise)
- Headphone mics (poor frequency response)

---

### Nice-to-Have: Wireless Lavalier Mic
**Cost:** $100-150
**Models:**
- RODE Wireless GO II ($150) — excellent range, USB-C charging
- Hollyland Lark 150 ($100) — budget wireless, good quality

**Pros:**
- No cable visible in shot
- Freedom of movement
- Easier for B-roll shots

**When to upgrade:** If filming multiple people or need cable-free shots. Not essential for single-person talking head.

---

### Voiceover Recording Setup
**Cost:** $50-100 (if using separate mic) or $0 (use lavalier)

**Option 1: Use lavalier mic in quiet room**
- Bedroom, closet, bathroom (tile reflects less than drywall)
- Hang blankets to dampen echo
- Record at night (less ambient noise)

**Option 2: USB condenser mic (alternative to lavalier)**
- Blue Yeti ($100) — industry standard for voiceover
- Audio-Technica AT2020 USB ($100) — excellent clarity

**Software:**
- Audacity (free, Mac/Windows) — record, edit, export
- GarageBand (free, Mac) — built-in noise reduction

**Recording tips:**
- Sit 6-12" from mic
- Use pop filter or foam windscreen (reduces plosives: "p", "b", "t")
- Record 5 seconds of silence at start (room tone for noise reduction)
- Do multiple takes of each section, choose best
- Export as 48kHz 24-bit WAV (DaVinci Resolve native format)

## Lighting

### Essential: Two Softbox Lights
**Cost:** $50-80 for pair
**Models:**
- Neewer 700W Softbox Lighting Kit ($60) — two 20"x28" softboxes, stands, bulbs
- LimoStudio 700W Photography Softbox ($50) — two softboxes, compact

**Setup for Talking Head:**
- **Key light:** 45° to left or right of camera, eye level
- **Fill light:** Opposite side, 45°, lower intensity (reduces shadows)
- **No backlight needed** (budget setup)

**Light positioning:**
1. Place key light 3-4 feet from subject, 45° angle
2. Place fill light opposite side, 4-5 feet away
3. Adjust intensity by moving lights closer/farther (softboxes diffuse harsh shadows)

**What to avoid:**
- Overhead lighting (creates shadows under eyes)
- Single light source (harsh shadows on one side)
- Window light only (inconsistent, changes throughout day)

---

### Nice-to-Have: 3-Point LED Lighting Kit
**Cost:** $150-200
**Models:**
- Neewer 660 RGB LED Panel Kit ($180) — dimmable, color temperature adjustable
- GVM RGB LED Video Light Kit ($200) — professional quality

**Pros:**
- Adjustable color temperature (match ambient light)
- Dimmable (fine-tune intensity)
- Cooler than tungsten bulbs (more comfortable for long shoots)

**When to upgrade:** If shooting multiple days (consistent color temperature), or need precise control over lighting mood.

---

### Lighting for Spirit Sphere Glow Shots

**Critical requirement:** LOW ambient light to maximize LED glow visibility.

**Setup:**
- Film in near darkness (<10 lux)
- Turn off all room lights, close blinds/curtains
- Use Spirit Sphere LED glow as PRIMARY light source
- Camera exposure: ISO 1600-3200, aperture f/2.8 or wider
- Black backdrop behind sphere (maximizes contrast)

**Optional accent light:**
- Single small LED panel ($30-50) at 10% intensity, behind and to side of sphere (rim light)
- Highlights sphere edges without washing out LED glow

**What to avoid:**
- Any bright ambient light (kills LED visibility)
- Flash photography (ruins glow effect)

## Backdrop

### Essential: Clean Wall
**Cost:** $0

Use existing neutral wall (white, gray, beige) as backdrop for talking head. Keep background simple — no clutter, distracting objects, or bright colors.

**Setup:**
- Position subject 3-4 feet from wall (reduces shadows)
- Ensure wall is evenly lit (no hotspots from lighting)

---

### Nice-to-Have: Black Backdrop
**Cost:** $20-40
**Models:**
- Neewer 6x9' Black Backdrop ($25) — collapsible, portable
- Savage Seamless Paper ($30-40) — studio-grade, black

**Use cases:**
- Spirit Sphere glow shots (maximizes contrast)
- Product shots (clean, professional look)
- PCB close-ups (black background makes components pop)

**Setup:**
- Hang backdrop on stand or mount to wall with clamps
- Ensure no wrinkles (iron or steam if fabric)
- Position subject/product 3-4 feet from backdrop (prevents shadows)

## Software

### Video Editing: DaVinci Resolve (FREE)
**Cost:** $0 (free version)
**Platform:** Mac, Windows, Linux

**Why DaVinci Resolve:**
- Professional-grade color grading (critical for enhancing LED glow)
- Built-in audio editing (Fairlight page)
- Motion graphics and VFX (Fusion page)
- Industry-standard export formats
- No watermark, no time limits

**Learning curve:** Moderate (2-3 hours to learn basics)

**Tutorials:**
- DaVinci Resolve official training (free on Blackmagic website)
- YouTube: "DaVinci Resolve Tutorial for Beginners" (10-20 minute videos)

**Alternative:** iMovie (Mac, free) — simpler but limited color grading and audio tools. NOT recommended for Spirit Sphere glow enhancement.

---

### Audio Editing: Audacity (FREE)
**Cost:** $0
**Platform:** Mac, Windows, Linux

**Use for:**
- Voiceover recording and editing
- Noise reduction (remove room hum, HVAC noise)
- Normalize audio levels
- Export as 48kHz 24-bit WAV for DaVinci Resolve import

**Alternative:** GarageBand (Mac, free) — built-in Mac app, easier UI but less precise control.

---

### Motion Graphics: DaVinci Resolve Fusion (FREE, built-in)
**Cost:** $0 (included in DaVinci Resolve)

**Use for:**
- Animated flow chart (How It Works section)
- Text overlays with animation
- Simple node diagrams

**Alternative:** After Effects (Adobe, $20/mo subscription) — more powerful but overkill for simple diagrams. Use Fusion to stay within budget.

## Camera Settings Cheat Sheet

### Talking Head (Well-Lit)
- **Resolution:** 4K (3840x2160) or 1080p (1920x1080)
- **Frame rate:** 24fps (cinematic) or 30fps (standard)
- **Exposure:** Manual mode, ISO 400-800
- **Aperture:** f/2.8-f/5.6 (subject in focus, background slightly blurred)
- **Shutter speed:** 1/50s (24fps) or 1/60s (30fps) — double the frame rate
- **White balance:** Auto or Daylight (5500K)
- **Focus:** Manual or continuous autofocus on subject's eyes

---

### Spirit Sphere Glow (Low Light)
- **Resolution:** 4K
- **Frame rate:** 24fps, 30fps, or 60fps (test which captures POV display best)
- **Exposure:** Manual mode, ISO 1600-3200
- **Aperture:** f/2.8 or wider (f/1.8 if available) — maximize light intake
- **Shutter speed:** 1/30s to 1/60s (slower = more light, but may blur motion)
- **White balance:** Manual 5500K-6500K (neutral LED color)
- **Focus:** Manual focus on LED sphere

**POV display capture:**
- **Challenge:** Persistence-of-vision effect may not translate to camera
- **Test:** Try 24fps, 30fps, and 60fps — one will likely capture POV display sharply
- **If flickering:** Adjust shutter speed to match LED refresh rate (trial and error)

---

### PCB Close-Up (Macro)
- **Resolution:** 4K
- **Frame rate:** 24fps or 30fps
- **Exposure:** Manual mode, ISO 400-800
- **Aperture:** f/5.6-f/8 (increases depth of field, keeps components sharp)
- **Shutter speed:** 1/50s or 1/60s
- **White balance:** Daylight (5500K)
- **Focus:** Manual macro focus
- **Lighting:** Two softbox lights at 45° angles, or single LED panel directly above

## Filming Checklist

### Pre-Shoot (Day Before)
- [ ] Charge all batteries (camera, phone, wireless mic)
- [ ] Clear memory cards (or free up phone storage)
- [ ] Test all equipment (camera, tripod, mic, lights)
- [ ] Set up filming location (clean desk, backdrop, remove clutter)
- [ ] Print shot list and script
- [ ] Rehearse talking head segments

---

### Day of Shoot
- [ ] Arrive early, set up lighting before golden hour fades (if using window light)
- [ ] Position camera on tripod, check framing
- [ ] Attach and test microphone (record 10s test, listen back)
- [ ] Turn off HVAC, fans, appliances (reduce noise)
- [ ] Turn off phone notifications (or use airplane mode)
- [ ] Lock camera exposure and focus
- [ ] Slate each shot (say shot number and take number on camera)
- [ ] Record 5 seconds of silence before and after each shot (room tone for editing)

---

### Post-Shoot
- [ ] Back up all footage immediately (two locations: external drive + cloud)
- [ ] Label files clearly (e.g., "TalkingHead_Take1.mp4", "SpiritSphere_Glow_Take3.mp4")
- [ ] Review footage same day (catch issues before breaking down set)
- [ ] Log which takes are keepers vs. reshoots needed

## Key Takeaways

1. **Audio > Video** — spend more on microphone than camera if forced to choose
2. **Lighting is free if you have softboxes** — window light is inconsistent, invest in controllable lights
3. **DaVinci Resolve is professional-grade and free** — no need for Adobe Premiere Pro subscription
4. **iPhone is sufficient** — modern iPhones shoot excellent 4K video, no need for expensive camera unless you want manual control
5. **Total budget: $200-600** — start minimal, upgrade as needed
6. **Do NOT buy gear you won't use** — no gimbal, no drone, no green screen
7. **Spirit Sphere glow shots are low-light photography** — high ISO, wide aperture, slow shutter, test frame rates
