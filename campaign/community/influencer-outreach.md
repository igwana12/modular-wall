# Tech YouTuber and Influencer Outreach Plan

Strategy for seeding prototypes to ESP32/maker/AI tech influencers 8 weeks before Kickstarter launch.

---

## Overview

**Goal:** Generate 5-10 organic video reviews from established tech YouTubers and maker influencers before the Kickstarter campaign launches.

**Why it matters:**
- 30-40% of hardware Kickstarter backers discover campaigns via YouTube reviews
- Trusted tech reviewers provide third-party validation (more credible than creator marketing)
- YouTube videos have long tail — continue driving traffic months after campaign ends
- Early reviews create social proof momentum for day-1 funding surge

**Budget:** $500-1,000
- 10 prototype units @ $50 BOM cost each = $500
- International shipping (5 units) = $200-300
- Packaging and instruction booklets = $100-200

**Timeline:** 8-week pre-launch outreach
- Week -8: Research channels, draft outreach email, prepare prototype units
- Week -6: Send cold outreach emails with prototype offer
- Week -5: Follow-up emails, ship units to interested channels
- Week -4 to -2: Reviewers test units, record videos
- Week -1: Videos publish with embargo lift, campaign launches

### What NOT to Say (Common Mistakes)

| Bad Approach | Why It Fails | Correct Approach |
|--------------|--------------|------------------|
| "Please review my product!" | Sounds desperate, no value proposition | "Would you be interested in covering an open-source ESP32 project?" |
| "This will make you money!" | Reviewers aren't affiliate marketers | "Free unit, honest review welcome" |
| "You have to review it by [date]" | Creates pressure, may decline | "Embargo lifts [date], but review anytime" |
| Generic mass email | Obvious copy-paste, no personalization | Reference specific video or project |
| "This is revolutionary!" | Overhyped, low credibility | "This is a Maker Edition — first hardware project, learning as I build" |
| Demanding video length or content | Reviewers hate being told what to say | "Cover whatever interests you — deep dive or overview, your call" |

## Reddit and Community Strategy (Organic Reach)

### r/esp32 Post Strategy

**Title:** [Show and Tell] The Orb — Open-Source ESP32-S3 Voice AI Oracle with 21 Greek Deities

**Post Format:**
```
Hi r/esp32,

I've been working on an ESP32-S3 voice AI project for the past 6 months and wanted to share it before launching on Kickstarter.

**What it is:**
- ESP32-S3-WROOM-1 (N16R8) running voice AI oracle conversations
- 21 Greek deities with unique voices (ElevenLabs TTS) and personalities (Claude LLM)
- WebSocket audio streaming (PCM 16-bit 16kHz) to backend
- RAG-based oracle readings (Pinecone vector search + mythology corpus)
- Optional POV volumetric LED display (APA102 strips, persistence-of-vision rendering)

**Tech stack:**
- INMP441 MEMS mic (I2S input)
- MAX98357A amplifier (I2S output)
- AssemblyAI Universal-3 Pro for STT
- ElevenLabs Flash v2.5 for TTS
- FastLED for POV display rendering

**Open-source:**
- Firmware: MIT license (GitHub link)
- PCB schematics: CERN-OHL-S-2.0
- 3D enclosure: CC BY-SA 4.0

**Demo video:** [YouTube link]

**GitHub:** [repo link]

Happy to answer technical questions — this is my first hardware project and I've learned a ton about ESP32 audio streaming, POV displays, and low-latency AI pipelines.

Kickstarter launches in [Month] if you want to support it, but the firmware and hardware files are already public.
```

**What to avoid:**
- Don't lead with "back my Kickstarter" (Reddit hates sales pitches)
- DO lead with technical details and open-source ethos
- Include GitHub link prominently (credibility signal)
- Engage with comments — answer technical questions, take feedback seriously

**Expected engagement:**
- 50-200 upvotes (if well-received)
- 20-50 comments (mostly technical questions)
- 1,000-3,000 impressions
- 5-10% click-through to GitHub or Kickstarter pre-launch page
