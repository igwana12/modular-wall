## 2. Multi-QR AR Technology

### Can Two QR Codes Be Detected Simultaneously?

**Yes, with caveats.** Two proven open-source frameworks support this:

#### MindAR (Recommended)

- Open source, MIT license, browser-based (no app install)
- Supports `maxTrack: 2` (or more) in the `mindar-image` attribute
- **Multi-Targets**: compile multiple target images, show different AR effects per target
- **Multi-Tracks**: detect and track multiple targets simultaneously in one camera session
- Works with A-Frame and Three.js
- Documentation: https://hiukim.github.io/mind-ar-js-doc/examples/multi-tracks/
- **Key limitation**: uses image tracking, not QR codes directly. The card art itself becomes the marker, not the QR code. This is actually BETTER for the combo mechanic — the entire card face is the trigger.

#### AR.js Multi-Marker

- Open source, well-documented multi-marker system
- "A multi-marker is basically a group of markers acting as one" — multiple markers create a unified coordinate space
- UCL Connected Environments built AR playing cards using exactly this approach: each card has a marker on the back, scanning multiple cards triggers combined experiences
- Reference project: https://connected-environments.org/making/ar-playing-cards/
- **Key limitation**: uses fiducial markers (black-and-white patterns), not image tracking. Less elegant than MindAR for card art.

#### 8th Wall (Commercial — Shut Down)

- Was the leading commercial WebAR platform
- **Shut down in 2026**. The ecosystem is fragmenting.
- Not a viable option.

#### Zapworks (Commercial — Active)

- Zappar's WebAR platform is still active
- Supports image tracking and "Zapcode" markers
- Commercial licensing required
- Could be used for premium experiences if budget allows

### Technical Architecture for Combo Detection

```
1. User opens camera via PWA (no app install needed)
2. MindAR tracks card face images (not QR codes)
3. Single card detected → show that god's myth AR scene
4. Second card enters frame → MindAR detects both targets
5. JavaScript logic: if (god_A + god_B) match a known combo → trigger combo scene
6. Combo AR renders between/above both physical cards
```

**QR codes remain for the reading experience** (scan QR → oracle reading). The AR combo detection uses the card art as image markers — a separate but complementary system.

### Feasibility Assessment

| Factor | Rating | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Technical feasibility | HIGH | MindAR multi-track is proven, open source, browser-based |
| User experience | MEDIUM | Requires holding phone steady over two cards; not trivial for casual users |
| Cross-device compatibility | MEDIUM | Works on iOS Safari and Android Chrome; performance varies on budget Android |
| Development complexity | MEDIUM | Need to compile .mind target files for all card images; combo logic is straightforward |
| No app install required | HIGH | WebAR runs in browser — critical for QR-scan-first flow |

### Recommendation

Use **MindAR with card art as image markers** (not QR codes) for combo detection. Keep QR codes for the oracle reading flow. Two separate entry points: QR scan for readings, camera for AR combos. This avoids the technical challenge of detecting two QR codes simultaneously and creates a richer experience where the art itself is the trigger.

## 3. TCG Economy Design

### How Major TCGs Handle Rarity and Packs

#### Pokemon (Benchmark: 100+ card sets)
- **Booster packs**: 10 cards per pack — 6 common, 3 uncommon, 1 rare
- **Rarity tiers**: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Holo Rare, Ultra Rare, Secret Rare
- **Set size**: 150-280 cards per expansion
- **Price**: $4.49 per booster, $36 for 10-pack bundle
- **Economy driver**: Chase cards at ultra-rare tier drive repeat purchases. "Pull rates" are the core dopamine loop.

#### Magic: The Gathering (Benchmark: complex economy)
- **Booster packs**: 15 cards — 10 common, 3 uncommon, 1 rare/mythic rare, 1 land
- **Rarity tiers**: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Mythic Rare
- **Secondary market**: Cards have real monetary value; some singles exceed $500
- **Economy driver**: Gameplay power differences between rarities create demand

#### Disney Lorcana (Benchmark: newer, simpler)
- **Booster packs**: 12 cards per pack
- **Rarity tiers**: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Super Rare, Legendary, Enchanted
- **Set size**: ~200 cards per set
- **Economy driver**: Disney IP drives collector behavior; foil "Enchanted" cards as chase items

### What Works for a Small Deck (21 Base + Expansions)

The critical insight from TCG economics research: **traditional printers require massive minimum orders to manually collate rare cards into packs**, which forces indie creators toward static "Starter Decks." This kills the unboxing/booster-pack dopamine loop.

#### Recommended Model: "Oracle Deck + Expansion Myth Packs"

**Base Product: The Oracle Deck ($29.99)**
- All 21 base god cards (one per god)
- Every buyer gets the complete base set — NO randomization at this tier
- This is the oracle reading product. It must be complete to function.

**Expansion: Myth Packs ($7.99 each)**
- 5 cards per pack
- Contains myth-specific variant cards (same gods, different stories/art)
- Guaranteed 3 common myths + 1 uncommon myth + 1 rare myth
- Some packs contain a "Legendary" three-god combo card (1 in 4 packs)
- **This is where the collectibility lives**

**Rarity Tiers for Myth Cards:**
| Tier | Count | Pull Rate | Description |
|------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Common | ~30 | 60% of pack slots | Single-god myths (e.g., Apollo and Daphne) |
| Uncommon | ~20 | 20% of pack slots | Two-god combos, well-known myths |
| Rare | ~15 | 15% of pack slots | Two-god combos, dramatic myths (Aphrodite + Ares + Net) |
| Legendary | ~5 | 5% of pack slots | Three-god combos (Persephone cycle, Judgment of Paris) |

**Why this works for 21 gods:**
- Base deck is NOT randomized — it is a complete product. No one feels cheated.
- Myth Packs add the TCG dopamine loop WITHOUT breaking the core oracle experience
- 21 gods can generate 70+ myth variant cards easily
- Trading incentive: "I pulled Zeus + Prometheus but I need Hades + Persephone"
- Small set means higher chance of "completing the collection" — more satisfying than Pokemon's 280-card sets where completion is near-impossible

### Key Indie TCG Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Do not randomize the base deck.** Oracle cards must be complete to function. Randomization belongs in expansions only.
2. **Print-on-demand cannot do randomized collation.** If using Game Crafter or MakePlayingCards, you get fixed decks. Randomized packs require a custom print run (minimum ~1,000 units per SKU through a TCG printer like Cartamundi or Legends of Card Gaming).
3. **Secondary market needs critical mass.** Trading only works with ~500+ active collectors. Until then, "completion tracker" in the app creates the same psychological pull.
4. **Foil/holographic variants are cheap at scale.** Hot-stamp foil adds $0.02-0.05/card at 5,000+ quantity. This is the easiest way to create chase cards.

### Recommendation

Launch with a complete 21-card base deck (not randomized). Introduce "Myth Packs" as the expansion product with TCG-style rarity. Target 50-70 myth variant cards for the first expansion. Use the digital completion tracker to drive desire before the physical secondary market reaches critical mass.

## 4. AR Card Games Precedent

### Drakerz-Confrontation (2013-2015) — FAILED

- **What it was**: French AR TCG where physical cards were scanned by a PC webcam to summon virtual creatures on screen
- **Kickstarter**: Sought $30,000 for US expansion
- **What went wrong**:
  - Required a PC with webcam — not mobile-first
  - The game was already partially available on Steam and their website before the Kickstarter, confusing backers
  - AR was the gimmick, not the game. The underlying card game was not compelling enough standalone
  - No mobile experience — this was 2014, before WebAR was viable
- **Lesson**: AR must enhance a product that already works without it. The oracle reading works without AR. AR is the bonus, not the core.

### Lightseekers / PlayFusion (2017-2022) — FAILED

- **What it was**: Cross-media platform — AR trading cards + action figures + mobile game + digital TCG
- **Kickstarter**: Successfully funded
- **What went wrong**:
  - Toys R Us bankruptcy killed their retail distribution weeks after launch
  - Too many product lines (cards + toys + digital) diluted focus
  - Management rushed products to market before they were ready
  - Employee morale collapsed; PlayFusion was eventually acquired by Build A Rocket Boy in 2024
- **Lesson**: Do one thing well before expanding. Cards first, AR second, hardware (Spirit Sphere) third. Do NOT launch multiple product lines simultaneously.

### Genesis Augmented Reality TCG (2024 — Active)

- **What it is**: AR collectible card game where cards are scanned to summon 3D monsters into an AR arena for real-time combat
- **Platform**: iOS and Android native app
- **Kickstarter**: Successfully funded
- **Key innovation**: Each individual card tracks XP, kill count, and levels up. If a card "dies," it loses XP — real stakes.
- **Featured by**: UniLad Gaming, Gaming Bible, Fraghero
- **Lesson**: AR TCG can work in 2024+ IF the AR adds genuine gameplay value (not just "look, 3D model appears"). The leveling/permadeath mechanic creates attachment to individual cards.

### Zappar / Zapworks Platform

- **Not a game, but a platform**: Zappar provides WebAR tools used by brands for card-triggered AR experiences
- **Used by**: Coca-Cola, Cadbury, various educational publishers
- **Relevance**: Proves the "scan physical card, see AR content" pattern works commercially
- **Lesson**: The platform exists and is battle-tested. The question is content quality, not technical viability.

### UCL Connected Environments — AR Playing Cards

- **What it was**: Academic project using AR.js multi-marker to create AR playing cards
- **Technical approach**: Each card has a marker on the back; scanning triggers audio/video AR experiences
- **Multiple markers detected simultaneously**: Yes, proven in this project
- **Lesson**: Multi-card AR detection is technically proven in an academic context. It works.

### Overall Pattern from AR Card Game History

| Factor | Failed Products | Successful Products |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| Core product | AR was the product | AR enhanced an existing product |
| Platform | PC/webcam only | Mobile-first (native or WebAR) |
| Standalone value | Cards useless without tech | Cards had value without tech |
| Focus | Multiple product lines | One product, done well |
| Distribution | Dependent on retail | Direct-to-consumer + Kickstarter |

### Recommendation

The Oracle Cards concept is correctly positioned: the oracle reading is the core product (works without AR), and AR combo reenactments are the enhancement layer. This matches the pattern of successful AR card products. The key risk is scope creep — resist the temptation to launch AR, combo mechanics, and the Spirit Sphere simultaneously.

## 5. Mythology-Specific AR

### Existing Products

#### "AR The Gods of Olympus" (Academic, 2023)

- **What**: AR educational game for teaching Greek mythology to 3rd graders in Greece
- **Published**: MDPI Multimodal Technologies and Interaction journal (2023)
- **Approach**: Mini-games including an AR game with Olympian gods using narration and memory mechanics
- **Target**: Primary school students
- **Relevance**: Proves the concept of "Greek gods + AR" has been validated in educational research. No one has commercialized it as a consumer product.
- Source: https://www.mdpi.com/2414-4088/7/1/2

#### JOIST Innovation Park — Greek Mythology AR + 3D Printing

- **What**: Educational program using 12 AR applications and 3D printing to teach Greek mythology
- **Target**: Children
- **Approach**: Each of the 12 Olympian gods has an AR experience
- **Relevance**: Confirms the "one AR experience per god" pattern is natural and intuitive

#### FourtheDesign — AR in Greek Tourism

- **What**: AR applications for tourism in Greece, bringing ancient sites to life
- **Approach**: On-site AR overlays showing reconstructed ancient buildings and mythological scenes
- **Relevance**: AR + Greek mythology is actively developed in tourism. No one has done it as a collectible card product.

### The Gap in the Market

Nobody has combined:
1. Physical collectible cards
2. Greek mythology content
3. AR experiences triggered by card scanning
4. AI-personalized oracle readings
5. Combo mechanics between cards

Each element exists separately. The combination is novel. The closest competitor is generic "AR educational cards" on Amazon (dinosaurs, animals, space), which have zero narrative depth and no AI personalization.

### Recommendation

The mythology + AR space is wide open for a premium consumer product. Educational products exist but are institutional/academic. Tourism AR exists but is location-bound. No one has built a portable, collectible, mythology AR experience with AI personalization. This is a genuine whitespace opportunity.

## 6. Production Scaling

### The Art Problem

If each myth needs unique art:
- **21 base god cards**: Already have PANTHEON art (525 panels from Midjourney)
- **~70 myth variant cards**: Each needs a unique scene depicting a specific myth
- **Total new art needed**: ~70 scene illustrations

### AI Art Generation at Scale (2026 State of the Art)

#### FLUX Kontext (Recommended for Consistency)

- **Style transfer via reference images**: Feed one PANTHEON art piece as style reference, generate myth scenes in the same aesthetic
- **Multi-reference system**: Combine up to 10 images to anchor visual direction
- **Cost**: Mid-tier pricing; suitable for 70+ images at production quality
- **Consistency**: FLUX Kontext is currently the best tool for "generate many images in one consistent style"

#### Midjourney V7

- **Advantage**: Already used for the PANTHEON art. Same tool = same aesthetic DNA
- **Image-to-image**: V7 handles style preservation better than previous versions
- **Cost**: $10-120/month depending on plan; generating 70 images requires ~500-700 generations (accounting for rejects and iterations), achievable on a Standard plan ($30/month) in 2-3 weeks
- **Recommendation**: Use Midjourney V7 with `--sref` (style reference) pointing to existing PANTHEON art to maintain visual consistency

#### Production Pipeline

```
1. Create a "style bible" — 5-10 PANTHEON reference images that define the look
2. For each myth scene, write a detailed prompt including:
   - Which gods appear (using character references from base cards)
   - The specific mythological moment (e.g., "Hephaestus catching Aphrodite and Ares in the golden net")
   - Art style anchored to PANTHEON references
3. Generate 5-10 variants per myth scene
4. Human curation: select best variant, light touch-up in Photoshop
5. CMYK conversion and print preparation
```

#### Cost Estimate for 70 Myth Scene Cards

| Item | Cost | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Midjourney Standard Plan | $30/month x 2 months | ~500 GPU minutes covers 700+ generations |
| FLUX Kontext (backup) | $0-50 | Open source; or hosted API credits |
| Photoshop touch-up labor | 1-2 hours per card x 70 = 70-140 hours | At $0 (self) or $25/hr freelance = $1,750-3,500 |
| CMYK conversion + print prep | Included in Figma workflow | Already established for base cards |
| **Total estimate** | **$60-3,560** | Self-production on the low end; freelance touch-up on the high end |

#### Timeline Estimate

- **AI generation**: 2-3 weeks (batched, ~5 cards/day including iterations)
- **Curation and touch-up**: 2-3 weeks (can overlap with generation)
- **Print preparation**: 1 week
- **Total**: 5-7 weeks for 70 myth variant cards

### Key Risk: Consistency

The biggest risk is NOT cost or time — it is **visual consistency across 70+ images**. AI generation can drift in style between sessions. Mitigation:

1. Use `--sref` (Midjourney) or reference image anchoring (FLUX) on EVERY generation
2. Batch by god — generate all Zeus myths together, all Athena myths together
3. Maintain a "reject pile" and re-generate from the same style anchor
4. Final pass: human eye checks all 70 cards side-by-side for style coherence

### Recommendation

AI art generation makes 70+ unique myth scenes feasible within the $2-5K pre-Kickstarter budget and 5-7 week timeline. Use Midjourney V7 with style references to the existing PANTHEON art. The production pipeline is: generate, curate, touch-up, print-prep. The risk is style drift, mitigated by strict reference anchoring and batched generation.

## Verdict: Go / No-Go Assessment

### Overall Assessment: CONDITIONAL GO

The combo AR mythology concept is technically feasible, commercially differentiated, and artistically achievable within budget. However, it should be treated as a **v2 expansion product**, not a v1 launch feature.

### What Makes This a Go

1. **Technical feasibility is proven.** MindAR multi-track detection works in the browser. No app install required. The UCL AR Playing Cards project demonstrated multi-card AR detection in production.

2. **The myth combo mapping is rich.** 46 natural two-god pairings and 19 three-god pairings from real Greek mythology — this is not forced. The source material demands this product.

3. **The TCG economy model is viable** for a small deck. Complete base deck + randomized Myth Packs avoids the indie TCG trap of randomizing the core product.

4. **AI art generation solves the scale problem.** 70+ unique myth scene cards are producible in 5-7 weeks for under $3,500 using Midjourney V7 with style references.

5. **No direct competitor exists.** Greek mythology + AR + collectible cards + AI readings = genuine whitespace.

6. **Failed AR card games failed for the right reasons** — they made AR the product instead of the enhancement. Oracle Cards avoids this by making the oracle reading the core and AR the bonus.

### What Makes This Conditional (Not Unconditional Go)

1. **Scope risk is the primary threat.** The base Oracle Cards product (21 cards, QR readings) is not yet shipped. Adding combo mechanics, AR, and 70+ variant cards before validating the core product is premature.

2. **AR user experience is fiddly.** Holding a phone steady over two cards while they trigger a combined experience is not effortless. Needs extensive UX testing.

3. **Randomized packs require custom print runs.** Game Crafter and MakePlayingCards cannot do randomized collation. Myth Packs need a TCG-specific printer (minimum ~1,000 units per SKU). This is a $5,000-15,000 commitment.

4. **Trading only works at scale.** The social combo mechanic ("I have Zeus, you have Hera, let's see their myth together") requires ~500+ active collectors. Before that threshold, it is a solo completionist experience.

### Recommended Next Steps

**Phase 1 (Now — Month 1-3): Ship the base product**
- Complete the 21-card Oracle Deck with QR readings
- Validate market demand with first 100+ sales
- Do NOT build AR yet

**Phase 2 (Month 3-5): Prototype the AR combo experience**
- Build a MindAR proof-of-concept with 3 cards (Zeus, Hera, Athena)
- Test: single card = god's myth. Two cards = combo myth.
- Evaluate user experience: is it magical or frustrating?
- This is a weekend hack, not a production build

**Phase 3 (Month 5-8): Produce the Myth Pack expansion**
- If AR prototype is compelling, begin AI art generation for myth variant cards
- Start with 25 combo cards (top 20 two-god + top 5 three-god)
- Source a TCG printer for randomized Myth Packs
- Build digital completion tracker in the web app

**Phase 4 (Month 8+): Launch "Oracle Cards: Mythologica" expansion**
- 25 combo cards in randomized Myth Packs ($7.99 each)
- AR combo experiences for all 25 combos
- Marketing: "Your cards are only half the story"

### The One-Line Pitch

> "Each card tells one god's myth. Put two cards together and the full story comes alive in AR — but only if you have both."
