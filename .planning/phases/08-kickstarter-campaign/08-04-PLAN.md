Create campaign/video/ directory.

**script-30s.md** — 30-second demo clip script:
- **0-3s HOOK**: Close-up of Spirit Sphere glowing in a dark room, deity voice saying something striking (e.g., Zeus: "The path you seek was never hidden — you were simply afraid to look."). Sound design: ethereal ambient, then voice.
- **3-10s REVEAL**: Pull back to show the full Spirit Sphere on a desk. POV globe spinning with deity animation. "This is The Orb."
- **10-20s DEMO**: Quick cuts showing: (1) person speaking to Oracle Engine, (2) deity voice responding, (3) Spirit Sphere POV display animating during reading. Voiceover: "21 Greek deities. Real AI conversations. Open-source hardware."
- **20-27s PRODUCTS**: Side-by-side shot of Oracle Engine and Spirit Sphere. Text overlay: "Oracle Engine $79 / Spirit Sphere $179 — Early Bird"
- **27-30s CTA**: "Back us on Kickstarter" + URL. Spirit Sphere glow fades.

Tone: Mysterious, then wonder, then clarity. NOT tech-bro hype. The mythology creates the magic, the hardware makes it real.

**script-full.md** — 2-3 minute full campaign video, following research structure:
1. **HOOK (0-10s)**: Same Spirit Sphere reveal as 30s clip (reuse footage). Deity voice speaks. Viewer is intrigued.
2. **PROBLEM (10-30s)**: "For 3,000 years, Greek mythology has been locked in books and museums. The gods had voices, personalities, wisdom — but you could only read about them." B-roll: old books, museum statues, contrast with modern tech. "What if you could actually speak with them?"
3. **SOLUTION DEMO (30-90s)**: Show Oracle Engine first (accessible product). Person asks a question, pauses, Zeus responds with personalized oracle reading. Then show Spirit Sphere — same interaction but now with POV deity animation. "21 deities, each with their own voice, personality, and oracle wisdom. Powered by the same AI that drives modern language models, but wrapped in 3,000 years of mythology." Show 2-3 different deities briefly (Zeus, Athena, Aphrodite).
4. **HOW IT WORKS (90-120s)**: Brief tech overview with animated diagram or text overlays. "ESP32-S3 microcontroller. Open-source firmware. Connects to your WiFi, talks to our oracle backend. Your voice goes in, a deity's voice comes out." Show the PCB briefly. "And yes — it is fully open-source. Flash your own firmware, remix the enclosure, build your own deity."
5. **MAKER STORY (120-150s)**: Talking head segment. "I'm [name], and this is a Maker Edition. I'm not pretending this is a polished consumer product — it is a working piece of mythological technology, built by a one-person team using the Sacred Circuits creative pipeline." Show quick montage of PANTHEON art, card deck, reading experience. "The cards, the voices, the readings — they already exist. The Orb just gives them a physical home."
6. **TIERS AND CTA (150-180s)**: Show both products with pricing. "Oracle Engine: voice AI oracle, $79 early bird. Spirit Sphere: the full experience with holographic display, $179 early bird. Both include the 21-card oracle deck and lifetime digital access." End on Spirit Sphere glowing. "Back The Orb on Kickstarter."

Include parenthetical stage directions: (CLOSE-UP), (WIDE), (B-ROLL), (TEXT OVERLAY), (VOICEOVER), (TALKING HEAD).

Apply McKee principles from CLAUDE.md: the video has a VALUE REVERSAL (ancient locked away -> technology unlocks it), ESCALATION (simple voice -> full sphere), and CONTROLLING IDEA (mythology deserves a living voice, not a museum case).

- 30s clip: ~8-10 shots
- Full video: ~20-25 shots
- Mark each shot as: READY (can film without hardware), NEEDS_HARDWARE (requires prototype), or POST_PRODUCTION (animation, overlay, editing)

**shot-list.md** — Categorized shot list extracted from storyboard:

**READY NOW (no hardware needed):**
- Talking head segments (maker story, CTA)
- B-roll: books, mythology imagery, PANTHEON art on screen
- Screen recordings: oracle reading web experience, digital guidebook
- Text overlay plates (typography, pricing cards)
- Blender animations: exploded hardware view, tech diagram

**BLOCKED (needs working hardware):**
- Spirit Sphere glowing / POV display in action
- Oracle Engine on desk responding to voice
- PCB close-up shots
- Assembly process footage
- Deity voice conversation demonstration

**POST-PRODUCTION (editing, no hardware):**
- Color grading, sound design
- Music selection (royalty-free ambient/ethereal)
- Text overlays and motion graphics
- DaVinci Resolve editing

For each blocked shot: note which hardware component is needed (ESP32 prototype? POV display? Both?) and when it is expected per production-timeline.md.

**gear-and-setup.md** — DIY production under $2K per research:
- Camera: iPhone (current model) in horizontal, or borrow/rent a mirrorless ($0-200)
- Tripod: $30 basic tripod
- Audio: Lavalier mic $50-100 (RODE smartLav+ or similar) — audio quality > video quality
- Lighting: Two softbox lights ($50-80 total) for talking head. Desk lamp for product shots.
- Background: Clean desk or table against neutral wall. Black backdrop for Spirit Sphere glow shots.
- Software: DaVinci Resolve (free) for editing. Blender (free) for 3D exploded views.
- Music: Epidemic Sound or Artlist subscription ($15/mo) OR royalty-free (Kevin MacLeod, Pixabay)
- Total estimated budget: $200-400 for gear (most reusable)
- Setup tips: shoot multiple angles of talking head segments, get more footage than needed, Spirit Sphere demos in low ambient light for maximum glow effect

**influencer-outreach.md** — Tech YouTuber outreach plan per KS-08:
- Target channels (research and list 10+):
  - Andreas Spiess (ESP32 expert, 500K+ subs)
  - Random Nerd Tutorials (ESP32 tutorials, 200K+ subs)
  - Great Scott! (electronics projects)
  - DroneBot Workshop (maker projects)
  - Unexpected Maker (ESP32 focused)
  - Jeff Geerling (maker/hardware, huge reach)
  - Zack Freedman (maker community)
  - element14 Presents (electronics community)
  - Hackster.io (platform, not channel — submit project)
  - r/esp32 moderators (Reddit, not YouTube — community post)
- For each: subscriber count, content focus, why they are relevant, contact method (email from About page, or DM)
- Outreach template: brief email introducing The Orb, offer prototype unit for review, include GitHub repo link for credibility, offer exclusive early look
- Timeline: reach out 8 weeks before launch, send units 4-6 weeks before, embargo lifts 1 week before campaign
- Budget: 10 prototype units (~$500 at BOM cost + shipping)
- Include pitch angle: "open-source ESP32 voice AI with mythology twist" — appeals to both maker AND AI audiences
