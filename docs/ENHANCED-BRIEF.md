## Core Thesis

### As Software Ate the World, AI Is Going to Throw It Back Up

Software took the physical world and compressed it behind glass. The globe on your desk became Google Maps. The clock on your wall became a phone widget. The photo wall became Instagram. The stereo became Spotify. The calendar, the weather station, the health monitor — all eaten by apps, all trapped inside a 6-inch rectangle.

Now AI dissolves the barrier that kept them there. The programming knowledge that locked hardware to specialists? Gone. A 16-year-old can describe what they want, AI writes the firmware, they print the case, and snap a physical module onto their wall. **The things the phone stole from the physical world — we're giving them back. And now they're alive.**

### The First Wavers Built This Bridge

> *"We grew up analog but came of age digital. Born between like 1975 and 1980. We played outside as kids, climbed trees, scraped our knees. But we also had Nintendo. We grew up with vinyl records and cassette tapes, but we also got our first email in college. We have one foot in each world."*
> — Tarquin, Chapter 7: Tulum — Zero Equals One

This is the generation that understands both sides. Kids younger than us can't change a bike tire — they never lived in the analog world. People older than us can't use computers — they never crossed into the digital. But the **First Wavers** (born ~1975–1985) have one foot in each world. They recorded on vinyl AND burned CDs. They wrote letters AND sent emails. They played outside AND played Nintendo.

This generation — the first youth of the digital global village — is uniquely positioned to build the bridge back. Not by rejecting the digital, but by giving digital intelligence a physical body. A wall you build with your hands that thinks with AI. Analog construction, digital soul.

> *"A global awakening. A generation of kids who'd grown up analog learning to navigate digital. Learning to merge the ancient and the modern. Learning to be citizens of the global village. We were the First Wavers."*

### The Wall Is That Bridge

The wall is where the analog human body meets the digital AI mind. Not through a headset. Not through a phone. Through a physical surface you stand in front of, interact with, and build with your own hands.

A clock module. A globe with live satellite data. A speaker that plays spatial audio. A hologram that shows your heartbeat. An LED matrix that visualizes CO2 emissions. A camera that captures your motion and feeds it to a game engine. **Each module is an app that escaped the phone and became physical again** — but now it's programmable, connected, intelligent, and built by you.

**Target**: First Wavers and the generations they're raising — curious tinkerers, creative technologists, parents building with kids. Ages 14 to 50. Gamers, IoT hobbyists, crypto/tech enthusiasts, makers, content creators, students. Anyone who wants to understand AI by building something physical that uses it.

## One-Line Pitch

A modular, snap-together wall computer — your desktop, but surrounding you on a wall. Mixed screens, holograms, speakers, sensors, cameras, and lights that capture ideas, inspire, and provide. Built from open hardware you assemble yourself.

## What Changed From v1

The original vision was "smart Nanoleaf with holograms." After deep research and ideation, this has evolved into something fundamentally different:

- **Not a display wall** — it's a human-computer interface for the AI era
- **Not just output** — it captures voice, motion, video, and reflects structured ideas back
- **Not JARVIS** — the wall has its own lightweight controller agent (a display compositor, not a conversational AI)
- **Education is core** — the wall teaches you IoT/AI as you build it
- **Transparent aesthetics** — see-through acrylic panels, internal LED glow, electronics as art
- **Handheld controller** — R1-style companion device for physical interaction
- **Health + wellness** — medical IoT dashboard integration
- **Creative capture studio** — the wall sees you, hears you, and feeds your ideas back
- **AR/VR bridge** — configurable from a headset, serves as spatial anchor, persists creative state between headset-on and headset-off

---

## AR/VR Integration

The wall and AR/VR headsets are **complementary, not competitive**:

- **Configure the wall from VR** — spatial UI in a headset may be faster than an app for arranging modules, setting scenes, routing content. Drag-and-drop in 3D space.
- **Spatial anchor** — the wall's glowing modules are trackable landmarks in mixed reality. You always know where you are relative to the wall.
- **Passthrough extension** — Apple Vision Pro / Quest 3 passthrough sees the physical wall. AR overlays extend panels into 3D space. A deity on the hologram fan steps off the wall and walks the room.
- **Creative handoff** — design in VR, take the headset off, work continues on the wall panels. Sketch on paper, wall camera captures it, put headset on and it's in your VR workspace.
- **Combined mocap** — wall's depth camera (front) + headset tracking (head/hands) = better full-body capture than either alone.
- **Social window** — visitors without headsets see the wall. The person in VR sees the extended version. Same session, different views.

**The wall is home base. The headset is the expedition. You always come back to the wall.**

## The 10 Value Propositions

### 1. Ambient Light System
LED panels that shift with circadian rhythm, weather, mood, time of day. Warm sunrise gradient in the morning, cool blue for focus, warm amber at night. Addressable LED strips inside semi-transparent enclosures create a living light sculpture.

### 2. Audio System
Distributed speakers across the wall — tweeters, full-range drivers, surface exciters that make panels vibrate as speakers, and a subwoofer module. Connects to Spotify, Apple Music, podcasts, RSS feeds. Spatial audio from multiple positions creates immersive soundscapes.

### 3. Smart Home Dashboard
Plugs into Home Assistant, Matter, Thread. Control all smart devices from the wall. Visual status of lights, locks, thermostat, cameras. The wall IS your smart home interface — not an app on your phone.

### 4. Health & Wellness Monitor
Pulls data from Apple Health, Fitbit, Oura Ring, Withings, any medical IoT device. Displays:
- Sleep quality and patterns
- Heart rate trends
- Calorie burn and nutrition
- Exercise and step counts
- Custom health metrics

Beautiful visual interfaces that change throughout the day — morning shows sleep summary, evening shows activity recap.

### 5. Mixed Media Art Surface
Holograms, pixel art, edge-lit sacred geometry, video, projections, e-ink art frames. Multiple display technologies in one system creating experiences no single device can match.

### 6. Education Platform
**The wall teaches you how to build and control IoT devices with AI.** This is a massive differentiator. You're not just selling a wall — you're selling the journey of building it. Each module you add teaches:
- Electronics fundamentals (power, signals, protocols)
- 3D printing (enclosure design)
- Networking (ESP-NOW, WiFi, CAN bus, Matter)
- AI integration (voice control, computer vision, orchestration)
- Programming (Arduino, MicroPython, LVGL)

The wall grows with your knowledge.

### 7. Information Display
Calendar, weather, news, stocks, transit, social media — like Tidbyt but modular and multi-screen. Different panels show different data. E-ink modules for always-on low-power info. LED matrices for scrolling tickers.

### 8. Handheld Controller
An R1-style companion device — small, physical, with a screen — that controls the wall. More than a phone app. Configure modules, switch scenes, rearrange what's displayed where. Could be built from an ESP32 with a small screen + rotary encoder.

### 9. Creative Capture Studio
**The wall is not just output — it's input.** This is the killer insight. The wall captures:
- **Voice** — microphone array transcribes your freestyle ideation in real-time
- **Body motion** — depth camera (Azure Kinect / similar) captures skeletal data for mocap, game characters, film animation, AI training
- **Gestures** — you articulate concepts physically, the wall captures and interprets
- **Photos/sketches** — camera modules digitize physical sketches, whiteboards, reference materials
- **Video** — record yourself presenting, pitching, performing

And then reflects it all back:
- Transcribed ideas appear as structured notes (synced to Obsidian)
- Body motion renders as 3D skeleton preview on a screen panel
- Captured sketches appear digitized alongside AI-generated variations
- Your freestyle becomes a draft, outline, or storyboard

**The creative loop**: You stand in front of your wall surrounded by inspiration — images, videos, Obsidian notes, reference boards across multiple panels. You start talking, freestyling. The wall captures everything, processes it through AI, and feeds structured output back to the displays. Input becomes output becomes input. The wall is your creative mirror.

**Use cases**:
- **Game developer**: Capture body motion for character animation, preview in real-time
- **Film director**: Storyboard ideas vocally, see them visualized as panels
- **Writer**: Freestyle plot ideas, see them structured into narrative arcs
- **Musician**: See audio waveforms, lyrics, mood visualizations while recording
- **Designer**: Pin reference images, sketch alongside, see AI variations generated live
- **Content creator**: Record yourself, see analytics and content suggestions on surrounding panels

### 10. External Hardware Integration
The wall connects to existing smart devices and platforms:
- **Google Nest** — thermostat, cameras, speakers, displays
- **Apple HomeKit** — via Matter/Thread bridge
- **Xbox Kinect / Azure Kinect DK** — depth sensing, volumetric capture, skeletal tracking
- **Ring / security cameras** — display feeds on screen modules
- **Sonos / HomePod** — integrate with existing audio
- **Smart plugs, lights, locks** — via Home Assistant
- **Medical IoT** — blood pressure monitors, glucose monitors, sleep trackers
- **3D printers** — monitor print progress on a panel
- **Weather stations** — display local sensor data

### 11. Service Widgets — The Spatial App Launcher
The wall is a **physical app launcher** — voice-activated, AI-routed, contextually aware. You don't pull out your phone. You speak, and the relevant service appears on the right screen panel at whatever size makes sense. When done, it dismisses and returns to ambient mode.

**How it works**: "Book me a table at Nobu for Friday" → AI confirms → OpenTable widget renders on a screen panel → you tap "8pm, party of 4" → confirmed → widget dismisses.

**Service integrations via open APIs**:
- **Travel**: Uber/Lyft (live map), Expedia/Booking (hotels), airline apps (flight status, boarding pass on e-ink panel)
- **Food**: OpenTable (reservations), DoorDash/Uber Eats (order tracking map)
- **Finance**: Bank balance, crypto portfolio, stock watchlist
- **Communication**: Slack messages, email preview, calendar next-up
- **Shopping**: Amazon order tracking, package delivery map
- **Entertainment**: Netflix/YouTube queue, Spotify now-playing visualizer

**Contextual awareness**: The AI knows you have a flight in 3 hours → proactively shows flight status + weather at destination + Uber ETA to airport, each on a different panel. No prompt needed.

### 12. Content Curation & Digital Wellness — The Anti-Addiction Interface

The wall does what phones never will: **regulate your content intake.**

Your phone's algorithm wants infinite scroll. The wall does the opposite: each content channel gets its own brick, its own time allocation, its own AI filter. When time's up, the brick goes dark.

**How it works**:
- **Channel bricks**: TikTok brick, YouTube brick, Instagram brick, News brick — each is a dedicated screen module for one content source
- **Time-boxed**: You allocate 30-45 minutes per channel per day. The brick turns on at your scheduled time, shows AI-curated content, then turns off. No rabbit hole.
- **AI-curated**: From 500 posts in your feed, AI selects the 15 that actually match your interests. No engagement bait, no rage content, no algorithm manipulation.
- **Separated silos**: Each channel is physically separate. TikTok can't bleed into your news feed. Work content stays on the work brick. No algorithmic crossover.
- **Save for later**: See something interesting but out of time? Tap to save it — it appears on your wall's "read later" panel at a time you choose.

**The creative lifecycle on one surface**:
```
INTAKE (regulated)  →  PROCESS (brainstorm)  →  OUTPUT (create)  →  SHOWCASE (display)
Curated feeds          Sketches, voice notes     Writing, design      Finished work
Time-boxed channels    AI-structured ideas       Code, video, art     Portfolio on wall
Filtered inspiration   Mind maps on panels       3D models, music     Art that evolves
```

The wall becomes a **sketchbook, notebook, creative workbench, and gallery** — a place to regulate intake, organize inspiration, brainstorm, create, and then display the results. The full creative cycle in one physical surface, with healthy boundaries that no phone will ever enforce.

**Anti-patterns the wall prevents**:
- Infinite scroll (time limit enforced physically — brick turns off)
- Context switching (each channel is a separate physical object)
- Algorithm manipulation (AI curates FOR you, not for advertisers)
- Late-night doom scrolling (wall can enforce bedtime mode — all content bricks go dark)

## Architecture Overview

### System Layers

```
[Handheld Controller]  [Phone App]  [Voice]  [JARVIS]  [Home Assistant]
         |                  |          |          |            |
         +------------------+----------+----------+------------+
                            |
                    [Wall Controller Agent]
                    (Display compositor / router)
                            |
                    [Central Compute Module]
                    (Orange Pi 5+ or Jetson Orin Nano)
                            |
              +-------------+-------------+
              |             |             |
         [CAN Bus]    [ArtNet/WiFi]   [ESP-NOW]
              |             |             |
    +---------+---------+   |   +---------+---------+
    |    |    |    |    |   |   |    |    |    |    |
   [M1] [M2] [M3] [M4] [M5]  [M6] [M7] [M8] [M9] [M10]
   Screen Holo Glow Speaker Sense Camera Pixel eInk Round Arm
```

### Wall Controller Agent (NOT JARVIS)

A lightweight, dedicated software agent that:
- **Routes content to panels** — weather data → screen module, ambient color → glow module, music visualizer → LED matrix
- **Manages module discovery** — detects what's connected, where, what type (via I2C EEPROM on each module)
- **Handles scenes/presets** — morning mode, focus mode, movie mode, sleep mode
- **Exposes a simple API** — any system (JARVIS, Home Assistant, phone app, handheld controller) can push content to specific modules
- **Manages scheduling** — different content at different times of day
- **Handles health data routing** — pulls from health APIs, formats for display modules

Think of it as a **display compositor / window manager for a physical wall** — not a conversational AI. It's a client that any AI can talk to.

### Physical System

**Backplane**: 3D-printed PETG rail with embedded neodymium magnets + 6-pin pogo connectors per slot:
- Pin 1: 5V power
- Pin 2: GND
- Pin 3: CAN_H (data bus)
- Pin 4: CAN_L (data bus)
- Pin 5: SDA (I2C for module discovery)
- Pin 6: SCL (I2C for module discovery)

**Module identity**: Each module has an I2C EEPROM with a type ID. When a panel snaps into the rail, the central compute reads the EEPROM and knows what type of module just connected.

**Power**: Mean Well RSP-150-5 (5V/30A/150W) feeds the backplane bus. Each module draws 0.5–3W depending on type.

### Bits to Pieces — Recycled Plastic Story

Inspired by William McDonough's *Cradle to Cradle* ("This Book Is Not a Tree" — a book printed on recycled plastic polymers because plastic is the right material for something you want to last forever).

**The concept**: Print wall modules from reclaimed ocean plastic filament. Every brick is rescued plastic given a second life as part of your creative wall. The material's natural color variations and texture become part of the aesthetic — no two bricks look the same.

**Why it matters**:
- Sustainability story is tangible and physical — you can see and touch the recycled material
- Connects to Globe 2.0 (displaying climate data on an object made from the problem it visualizes)
- Opens partnerships with ocean plastic recyclers (Precious Plastic, Oceanworks, Plastix)
- Premium positioning — "made from ocean plastic" commands a price premium and press attention
- Educational angle — teaches about materials, recycling, circular economy alongside IoT/AI

**Supply chain**: Recycled PETG and PLA filaments from ocean plastic are commercially available (e.g., Refil, Reflow, 3D-Fuel). ~$30-40/kg vs $25/kg for virgin material — minimal cost premium.

## Aesthetic Vision

### The Look

The wall should look like a **cyberpunk art installation** — not a consumer electronics product. Key aesthetic principles:

1. **Transparency**: Smoke-tinted acrylic panels (#2064 Gray Smoke) let you see the electronics inside. The circuitry IS the decoration.

2. **Stealth Glow**: Adafruit Black LED Diffusion Acrylic — completely opaque black when off, but LEDs shine through when active. Panels appear to "wake up."

3. **Internal Illumination**: SK6812 RGBW LED strips inside enclosures create a warm glow through semi-transparent panels. Electronics float in colored light.

4. **Mixed Opacity**: Some panels are transparent (see the circuits), some are translucent (diffused glow), some are opaque (clean display surface). The mix creates visual depth.

5. **Edge Lighting**: Laser-engraved acrylic panels with LED strips on the edges — sacred geometry patterns that glow.

6. **Modular Grid**: Uniform panel size with magnetic snap attachment. The grid itself is part of the aesthetic — like a comic book panel layout.

## Key Technical Decisions

### Communication Protocol: CAN Bus (TWAI) over Backplane
- Built into every ESP32 — no external controller needed (just SN65HVD230 transceiver, $1)
- Differential signaling = noise-resistant over copper bus bars
- 112 nodes per segment, 1 Mbps
- Multi-master, arbitration built in
- Module discovery via I2C EEPROM when panel seats

### LED Control: ArtNet/sACN over WiFi
- Industry standard for LED fixture control
- WLED firmware on ESP32 supports both natively
- Central compute sends lighting commands to panel LEDs
- 16 universes at 40fps demonstrated on ESP32

### Module Identity: I2C EEPROM per Module
- When panel snaps into rail, I2C bus reads module type
- Central compute auto-configures what content to route there
- Hot-swappable — rearrange your wall anytime

### Panel Size Standard: TBD
- Needs to be standardized so all modules are interchangeable
- Likely 100x100mm or 120x120mm base grid
- Larger modules span multiple grid units (2x1, 2x2, etc.)

## Decisions Made (2026-04-05)

### Panel Design — Hardware-First Sizing
**No standardized grid.** Panels are squares and rectangles sized to house the specific hardware inside them. The approach:
1. Pick 2–3 hardware options per category (speakers, screens, holo fans, cameras, etc.)
2. Determine the physical dimensions of those components
3. Design casings around the hardware

### Enclosure Construction — Modular Frame System
- **3D-printed corners** — structural corner pieces that accept aluminum rods
- **Aluminum I-beam / extrusion rods** — engineering-grade rods plug into the 3D-printed corners to form the frame skeleton
- **Acrylic or 3D-printed panels** — slide or screw onto the frame for sides, front, and back. User chooses material (transparent, smoke, opaque, diffusion)
- **Board mounting** — chips/PCBs mount to panels via standoffs (3D-printable or purchasable)
- **Sourcing options**: Print yourself / third-party print service / buy pre-made from us

**Phase 5: Digital Twin (Ongoing)**

## Council Review Summary (5-Expert Analysis)

Full review: [COUNCIL-REVIEW.md](COUNCIL-REVIEW.md)

### The 3 Things Every Expert Agreed On

1. **Narrow before you widen.** Launch with ONE hero use case, TWO display types, ONE target customer. Expand after 1,000 users love that one thing.

2. **Pre-assemble electrical modules. Users do mechanical assembly only.** Sealed, tested modules that snap into the frame. Open-source STLs for customizers, but don't make structural integrity depend on user's printer calibration.

3. **The creative capture loop is the differentiator.** Dashboards and ambient light are table stakes. The depth-camera-to-stylized-avatar demo is the thing that makes people stop scrolling. Build ONE magical demo to perfection.

### Key Expert Recommendations
- **Hardware**: Injection-molded corners for sold kits. French cleat wall mount. Vibration isolation gaskets.
- **Market**: Reposition as "the open-source ambient computer." Kill 9 of 10 value props from launch messaging.
- **Education**: Ship 3 structured lesson plans with starter kit. QR-coded per module.
- **Interaction**: Three-zone model (far=ambient, mid=gesture+voice, near=touch+controller).
- **Manufacturing**: 100-unit pilot before Kickstarter. Starter kit = 2 display types only (LCD + LED matrix).
