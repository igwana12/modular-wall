# Section 1: The Thesis

## As Software Ate the World, AI Is Throwing It Back Up

Software took the physical world and compressed it behind glass. The globe on your desk became Google Maps. The clock on your wall became a phone widget. The photo wall became Instagram. The stereo became Spotify. The calendar, the weather station, the health monitor — all eaten by apps, all trapped inside a 6-inch rectangle.

**Now AI dissolves the barrier that kept them there.** The programming knowledge that locked hardware to specialists? Gone. A 16-year-old can describe what they want, AI writes the firmware, they print the case, and snap a physical module onto their wall.

**The things the phone stole from the physical world — we're giving them back. And now they're alive.**

### The First Wavers Built This Bridge

> *"We grew up analog but came of age digital. Born between like 1975 and 1980. We played outside as kids, climbed trees, scraped our knees. But we also had Nintendo. We grew up with vinyl records and cassette tapes, but we also got our first email in college. We have one foot in each world."*
>
> — Tarquin, Chapter 7: Tulum — Zero Equals One

This is the generation that understands both sides. Kids younger than us can't change a bike tire — they never lived in the analog world. People older than us can't use computers — they never crossed into the digital. But the **First Wavers** (born ~1975–1985) have one foot in each world.

This generation — the first youth of the digital global village — is uniquely positioned to build the bridge back. Not by rejecting the digital, but by giving digital intelligence a physical body.

**A wall you build with your hands that thinks with AI. Analog construction, digital soul.**

### The Wall Is That Bridge

The wall is where the analog human body meets the digital AI mind. Not through a headset. Not through a phone. Through a physical surface you stand in front of, interact with, and build with your own hands.

A clock module. A speaker that plays spatial audio. An LED matrix that visualizes your mood. A camera that captures your motion. **Each module is an app that escaped the phone and became physical again** — but now it's programmable, connected, intelligent, and built by you.

**Target**: First Wavers and the generations they're raising — curious tinkerers, creative technologists, parents building with kids. Ages 14 to 50. Gamers, IoT hobbyists, crypto/tech enthusiasts, makers, content creators, students. Anyone who wants to understand AI by building something physical that uses it.

# Section 2: Product Overview

## What Is mosAIc?

mosAIc is a modular, snap-together wall computer built from mixed screens, holograms, speakers, sensors, and lights. Each module magnetically attaches to a wall surface and self-configures. An AI conductor orchestrates content across all modules based on context, voice commands, and schedules.

**Open hardware. Proprietary soul.**

## 11 Value Propositions

### 1. Ambient Light System
LED panels that shift with circadian rhythm, weather, mood, time of day. Warm sunrise gradient in the morning, cool blue for focus, warm amber at night.

### 2. Audio System
Distributed speakers across the wall — spatial audio from multiple positions creates immersive soundscapes. Connects to Spotify, Apple Music, podcasts.

### 3. Smart Home Dashboard
Plugs into Home Assistant, Matter, Thread. Control all smart devices from the wall. The wall IS your smart home interface — not an app on your phone.

### 4. Health & Wellness Monitor
Pulls data from Apple Health, Fitbit, Oura Ring, Withings. Beautiful visual interfaces showing sleep quality, heart rate trends, exercise, and custom health metrics.

### 5. Mixed Media Art Surface
Holograms, pixel art, video, projections. Multiple display technologies in one system creating experiences no single device can match.

### 6. Education Platform
**The wall teaches you how to build and control IoT devices with AI.** Each module you add teaches electronics fundamentals, 3D printing, networking, AI integration, and programming. The wall grows with your knowledge.

### 7. Information Display
Calendar, weather, news, stocks, transit — like Tidbyt but modular and multi-screen. Different panels show different data.

### 8. Handheld Controller
An R1-style companion device — small, physical, with a screen — that controls the wall. More than a phone app.

### 9. Creative Capture Studio
The wall captures voice, body motion, gestures, photos/sketches, and video — then reflects it all back as structured output. Your creative mirror.

### 10. External Hardware Integration
Connects to Google Nest, Apple HomeKit, Xbox Kinect, Ring cameras, Sonos, medical IoT, 3D printers, weather stations.

### 11. Service Widgets
Voice-activated spatial app launcher. "Book me a table at Nobu for Friday" → AI confirms → OpenTable widget renders on screen → confirmed → dismisses. Uber, DoorDash, Spotify, Netflix — all physical, contextual, AI-routed.

# Section 4: How It Works

## Three Steps to Living Intelligence

### 1. Choose Your Modules
Start with a starter kit or build a custom configuration. Every wall is unique — yours reflects your life, your workflow, your aesthetic.

### 2. Snap Together
Magnetic pogo pin connectors. No wiring, no soldering, no programming. Modules snap to a wall-mounted surface and auto-configure. Hot-swappable — rearrange anytime.

### 3. AI Orchestrates
The Wall Controller Agent discovers each module, routes content intelligently, and creates choreographed scenes. Morning mode. Focus mode. Movie night. Sleep mode. The wall knows what you need before you ask.

# Section 6: Technical Architecture

## System Overview

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│        Wall Controller Agent            │
│     (FastAPI on Orange Pi 5+ / RPi)     │
│  - Module discovery (I2C EEPROM)        │
│  - Content routing engine                │
│  - Scene/preset management               │
│  - WebSocket API                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
                    │
         ┌──────────┴──────────┐
         │                     │
    CAN Bus               ArtNet/WiFi
    (wired backbone)      (wireless LED)
         │                     │
    ┌────┴─────────────────────┴────┐
    │                                │
┌───▼───┐ ┌────────┐ ┌────────┐ ┌──▼──┐
│Screen │ │ Pixel  │ │  Holo  │ │Glow │
│Module │ │ Module │ │ Module │ │Module│
└───────┘ └────────┘ └────────┘ └─────┘
```

## Communication Stack

- **CAN Bus (TWAI)**: Module commands over wired backplane (built into ESP32, SN65HVD230 transceiver at $1)
- **ArtNet/sACN**: LED control over WiFi (WLED firmware compatible)
- **I2C**: Module discovery via EEPROM
- **ESP-NOW**: Mesh sync for low-latency coordination

## Module Standard

- **Connector**: 4-pin magnetic pogo pins (Nanoleaf-style)
- **Power**: 5V via copper bus bar backbone
- **Signals**: CAN_H, CAN_L (or I2C SDA/SCL)
- **Safety**: PTC fuse ($0.10) + reverse polarity protection ($0.20) + TVS diode ($0.10) = $0.40 per module
- **Discovery**: I2C EEPROM stores module type ID, auto-configures on snap-in

# Section 7: Hardware Specifications

## Frame System
- **Extrusion**: MakerBeamXL 15x15mm aluminum
- **Corners**: 3D-printed PETG/ABS (50-100% infill for structural strength)
- **Wall Mount**: French cleat system from same extrusion stock
- **Front Panels**: Smoke acrylic (#2064 Gray Smoke) — see-through with internal LED glow
- **Diffusion**: Black diffusion acrylic (stealth glow — invisible when off, glows when on)

## Power System
- **PSU**: Mean Well RSP-150-5 (5V/30A, $40)
- **Distribution**: Copper bus bar backbone behind wall mounting plate
- **Connectors**: Magnetic pogo pin edge connectors (Adafruit DIY 4-pin or HytePro M402)
- **Short cables**: Colored USB-C cables for non-adjacent modules
- **Safety**: PTC fuses at each tap point

## Module Compute
- **Brain**: ESP32-S3 per module ($4-8)
- **Hub**: Orange Pi 5+ 16GB ($109) or Jetson Orin Nano ($249)

## Module Dimensions (Examples)
- Screen-S: 80x80mm
- Glow: 80x80mm or 120x120mm
- Pixel: 128x64mm (HUB75 panel)
- Holo: 120x120mm (fan diameter)
- Hub: 80x80mm
